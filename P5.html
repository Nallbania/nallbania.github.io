High-Fidelity Prototype

Final Prototype


We designed our high fidelty protoype using Adobe XD and made it interactive through Invision. Our color pallete featured a lavender/blue commonly associated with trust and calmness, and a yellow shade relating to happiness. Our main priorities with this design was to utilzie more recognizable icons, maintain consistency across pages, and provide a number of escape routes for users on every page.

Summative Evaluation

We conducted the same interview from the low-fidelity prototype for the high-fidelity prototype. We also added a system usability scale questionnaire to quantify how well people understood the prototype and how easy it was for them to use it. We also have users conduct the same tasks as described above. Across all of the interviews, there was notable improvement from the low-fidelity prototype with the gratefulness task. However, there were still a few remaining usability issues such as when some users got confused about how to exit certain pop-up windows or got distracted by certain visuals. Specifically, writing a message to your futureself section seemed to still be difficult to navigate. A few users did not understand what the envelopes represented, and others were unsure why they were writing a message.

Final Reflections

In the future, we would like to create a more in depth guide on the homepage to help teach people when they first use the app. Overall, our application has good memorability and efficiency, but the initial learnability could be vastly improved with more initial signifiers.
